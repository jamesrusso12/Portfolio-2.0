Database filter

My inaugural website marked a significant milestone in my development journey, as I successfully integrated MYSQL and PHP to establish a robust foundation. Employing Node.js, I seamlessly bridged the gap between my website and the PHP database, enabling a filtering mechanism driven by JavaScript. This multifaceted filter allowed users to sort data based on ascending or descending order, as well as refine results by names and games, exemplifying my commitment to creating a versatile and user-friendly platform.

Reactive Css Scoreboard

This screenshot depicts a leaderboard I designed, which harnesses data from my database to showcase the top 10 fastest completion times across all Halo games. I further enhanced its interactivity by incorporating CSS elements, making it responsive and dynamic, including hover effects for an engaging user experience.

D3.js reactive graphs

I utilized data stored within the PHP database to seamlessly integrate it with dynamic and responsive graphs powered by D3.js. This connection was established through JavaScript, allowing the graphs to automatically populate with the relevant information. While the possibilities for graph types were abundant, I carefully selected those that most effectively conveyed the primary objective: showcasing players who achieved the fastest completion times for their chosen game difficulties.

Versatility of D3.js graphs

In the screenshot, I incorporated a thoughtful selection of graphical representations, including two pie charts and a line graph. The top graph, a line graph, vividly illustrates the progression of player completion times over the years, expertly categorized by difficulty levels, each distinguished by distinct colors. This choice was made with user experience in mind, offering an engaging way for users to visualize the evolving landscape of player achievements throughout the years, showcasing impressive accomplishments across a diverse player base. Additionally, the two pie charts were a deliberate design choice, serving to underscore the remarkably close proximity in skill levels among these players. These charts effectively convey the minimal disparities in skill, highlighting the fine margins that set these players apart from one another.

Updated and Refined

The revamped website offers users a more engaging and interactive experience, shifting its focus from player completion times to the expansive realm of Halo maps, including community creations. Notable improvements include a direct connection to the database, introducing an edit and delete feature. With the insert option, users can seamlessly contribute their maps, fostering a collaborative community. The database encompasses a comprehensive collection of both classic maps and fan-made Forge creations. Users can easily update map details using the edit button, ensuring accuracy. Additionally, they have the flexibility to rectify errors and remove outdated maps using the delete feature, granting users control and fostering a vibrant, user-driven community around Halo maps.
